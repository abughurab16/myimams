The 4 Imams of Islam

The 4 Imams of Islam are the pious Muslim theologians and jurists who lived in the first three centuries of Islam, they were memorisers of the Quran Al-Kareem, well learned in the sciences of hadith and Faqihs(Experts on the sciences of Ijitihad).Today, most Muslims accept the ‘ijtihad’ of only these four Imams which are referred to as 'Madhabs' or 'Schools Of Thought'

Imam Nuʿmān ibn Thābit ibn Zūṭā ibn Marzubān or Abu Hanifa

Followers of this 'Madhab' are known as Hanafis

Imam Malik ibn Anas or Imam Malik

Followers of this 'Madhab' are known as Malikis

Imam Abu Abdullah Muhammad ibn Idris al-Shafi'i or Imam Al-Shafi'i

Followers of this 'Madhab' are known as Shafi'is

Imam Ahmad Ibn Hanbal

Followers of this 'Madhab' are known as Hanbalis
